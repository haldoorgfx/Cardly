Offline-first payment rails for low-connectivity regions

Dr. Amina Osman, Sahel Pay · Prof. Kwame Mensah, University of Ghana
Engineering & InfrastructureSubmitted Feb 04, 2026
paymentsoffline-firstdistributed systems
We present a payments architecture designed for intermittent connectivity, common across much of the Horn of Africa. By treating the local device as the source of truth and reconciling asynchronously, we achieve sub-second confirmation perceived latency while maintaining eventual consistency with the central ledger. We detail the conflict-resolution model, the cryptographic receipt chain that prevents double-spend during network partitions, and field results from a six-month pilot across 1,400 merchants in Djibouti and eastern Ethiopia. The pilot processed $2.3M in transactions with a 0.02% reconciliation failure rate.
